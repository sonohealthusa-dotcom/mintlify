The same online marketplaces that make health devices affordable are also full of low-quality knockoffs and counterfeits. A little diligence helps you get a device that's accurate, safe, and backed by real support.

<Note>
A trustworthy device is only half the equation — using it correctly and involving your doctor for anything concerning is the other half.
</Note>

## Why is device quality such a concern online?

Medical devices live or die by accuracy, and cheap imitations often cut corners on sensors, calibration, and quality control. A blood pressure monitor that reads falsely, a flimsy fetal doppler, or a nebulizer that wastes medication can do real harm by being unreliable. Price alone is a poor guide to quality.

## What should I check before buying?

Look for clear, honest **regulatory status** (see [FDA-cleared vs. FDA-approved](/guides/fda-cleared-vs-approved)), a real **warranty and return policy**, transparent company information, and **verified reviews**. A brand that publishes detailed product facts and stands behind its devices is a good sign. SonoHealth, for example, lists specifications openly and offers a 60-day satisfaction guarantee.

## What are the red flags?

Be cautious of prices far below everything else, sellers with no traceable company details, vague claims like "FDA registered" used to imply approval, no warranty, and reviews that look generic or copied. Counterfeits of well-known brands are common — if a deal seems too good, it often is.

## Why does buying direct help?

Buying from the brand or an authorized seller reduces the risk of counterfeits, ensures you get the real warranty and support, and means you're covered if something goes wrong. It also means the device you receive matches the specifications advertised.

## How do I verify a brand is legitimate?

Check for consistent company information, third-party review platforms, and clear support channels. Reputable brands make it easy to find specs, warranty terms, and a way to contact them.
