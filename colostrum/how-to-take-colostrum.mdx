Once you've decided to try bovine colostrum, the next questions are simple: when, how much, and what to mix it with. Here's a practical guide to fitting it into your routine.

<Note>
Bovine colostrum like SonoHealth's [Cowabunga](/colostrum/overview) is a dietary supplement, not a treatment. Follow the product label, and check with your doctor if you take medications, are pregnant or breastfeeding, or have a health condition.
</Note>

## When is the best time to take it?

There's no single required time. Many people take colostrum on an **empty stomach** — for example, first thing in the morning or between meals — on the theory that its bioactive proteins are best taken away from a large meal. Consistency day to day matters more than the exact hour.

## How much should I take?

Follow the dosing on your product's label, since concentration varies between products. If you're new to it, some people start at the lower end and increase gradually. Don't exceed the labeled amount without guidance.

## Can I mix colostrum powder into a drink?

Yes — colostrum powder can be stirred into cool or room-temperature water or a smoothie. Avoid very hot liquids, which may affect its delicate proteins. Mix until dissolved and drink it fairly soon after preparing.

## Can I take it with other supplements?

Colostrum is commonly paired with [probiotics](/colostrum/gut-and-immune-health) as part of a gut-health routine, and there's no well-known interaction. As a general rule, introduce one new supplement at a time so you can tell what's helping. If you take medications, ask your pharmacist about spacing.

## How long until I notice anything?

Responses vary widely. People who take colostrum for gut or immune support often give it a consistent trial of a few weeks before judging, while others notice little. Track how you feel, and set realistic expectations — it's a wellness supplement, not a quick fix.

## Who should check with a doctor first?

Anyone with a dairy or milk allergy, those who are pregnant or breastfeeding, parents considering it for a child, and anyone with a health condition should talk to a healthcare professional before starting.
