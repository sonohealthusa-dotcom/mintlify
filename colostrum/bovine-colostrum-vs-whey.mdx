Both bovine colostrum and whey come from milk, so they're easy to confuse — but they serve quite different purposes. Here's how they compare so you can decide what fits your goals.

## What is each one?

**Colostrum** is the first milk a cow produces in the days after giving birth — naturally rich in antibodies (immunoglobulins), growth factors, and proteins like lactoferrin. **Whey** is a protein fraction separated from regular milk, typically during cheesemaking, and is concentrated mainly for its protein content.

## What do people take them for?

- **Bovine colostrum** is usually taken for [gut and immune support](/colostrum/gut-and-immune-health) and general wellness, thanks to its bioactive components.
- **Whey protein** is taken mainly to **boost daily protein intake** for muscle building, recovery, and satiety.

In short: colostrum is about its bioactive compounds; whey is about protein quantity.

## Which has more protein?

Whey is the higher-protein choice per serving and is the better pick if your goal is simply to hit a protein target. Colostrum provides some protein but isn't intended as a primary protein source.

## Can I take both?

Many people do — for example, whey for protein and colostrum for gut or immune support — since they serve different roles. Introduce one new supplement at a time so you can tell how each affects you, and see [how to take colostrum](/colostrum/how-to-take-colostrum) for timing.

## A note on dairy sensitivity

Both are dairy-derived. If you have a milk allergy, neither is automatically safe — colostrum still contains milk proteins. People who are lactose-sensitive tolerate them differently. When in doubt, check with your doctor before starting.
