The AirPro's HEPA 14 filter is designed to last 6-12 months depending on usage and air quality conditions. The built-in smart tracking indicator takes the guesswork out of replacement timing, and the subscription option saves money while extending your warranty to lifetime coverage.

## When to Replace the Filter

The AirPro's smart lifetime tracking system monitors actual filter usage and will alert you when replacement is needed. General guidelines:

| Usage Pattern | Expected Filter Life |
|---|---|
| Light use (a few hours daily, clean environment) | 10-12 months |
| Moderate use (8-12 hours daily, typical home) | 6-9 months |
| Heavy use (24/7, pets, smokers, high dust) | 4-6 months |
| Wildfire smoke or construction | May need earlier replacement |

### Signs Your Filter Needs Replacement

- The smart tracking indicator signals replacement
- Noticeable decrease in airflow
- Persistent odors that the purifier no longer removes
- Increased noise at the same fan speed (motor working harder against a clogged filter)
- Visible discoloration or debris on the filter surface

## How the Smart Tracking Indicator Works

The AirPro does not use a simple timer to estimate filter life. The smart tracking system monitors:

- **Cumulative run time** across all speed settings
- **Air quality conditions** that accelerate filter wear
- **Fan speed patterns** -- higher speeds process more air and load the filter faster

When the indicator signals replacement, the filter has reached the point where efficiency begins to decline. Replacing the filter promptly ensures you continue getting HEPA 14 performance.

## Pre-Filter Maintenance

While the full filter assembly is replaced every 6-12 months, you can extend its life with regular pre-filter maintenance:

- **Vacuum the pre-filter** every 2-4 weeks to remove accumulated dust and debris
- **Use a soft brush attachment** to avoid damaging the filter media
- **Do not wash** the HEPA 14 or activated carbon layers -- water damages the filter media and destroys efficiency
- **Check for visible buildup** monthly, especially in homes with pets or during high-pollen seasons
- **Clean more frequently** during wildfire smoke events or construction nearby

Regular pre-filter maintenance prevents large particles from reaching the HEPA 14 layer prematurely, allowing the main filter to focus on the fine particles it is designed to capture.

## How to Replace the Filter

1. Turn off and unplug the AirPro
2. Remove the outer housing per the user manual instructions
3. Remove the used filter assembly
4. Insert the new replacement filter, ensuring proper orientation
5. Reassemble the housing
6. Plug in and turn on the AirPro
7. The smart tracking system resets automatically with the new filter

## Proper Filter Disposal

Used HEPA 14 filters contain captured pollutants, allergens, and potentially biological material. Dispose of used filters in a sealed bag with your regular household waste. Do not attempt to wash, vacuum, or reuse the HEPA 14 or activated carbon components.
