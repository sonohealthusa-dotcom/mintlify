## The Health Risk of Airborne Mold

Mold is more than a cosmetic problem. Airborne mold spores trigger allergic reactions, worsen asthma, and cause chronic respiratory issues. Black mold (Stachybotrys chartarum) is particularly concerning because it produces mycotoxins that can cause severe symptoms including persistent coughing, headaches, fatigue, and immune suppression.

Mold spores range from 1 to 30 microns in diameter, making them invisible to the naked eye but large enough for the AirPro's HEPA 14 filter to capture with 99.9% efficiency. Since the filter captures particles down to 0.1 microns, mold spores are well within its range.

## How the AirPro Fights Mold

### Stage 1: Pre-Filter

The pre-filter catches larger mold colony fragments and debris that become airborne when mold growth is disturbed. This prevents heavy particulate from clogging the primary HEPA 14 filter and maintains consistent airflow.

### Stage 2: HEPA 14 with Antibacterial Coating

The medical-grade HEPA 14 filter traps mold spores as small as 1 micron. The antibacterial coating on the filter surface inhibits microbial growth, preventing captured spores from colonizing the filter itself, a problem common with standard HEPA filters in humid environments.

### Stage 3: Activated Carbon + UV-C

The activated carbon layer absorbs musty odors and mycotoxin-laden VOCs that mold produces. The UV-C function provides a critical advantage: it kills captured mold spores on the filter, eliminating the risk of spores being released back into the air when the filter is disturbed or replaced.

## Why UV-C Matters for Mold

Standard air purifiers capture mold spores but leave them alive on the filter. In humid conditions, these spores can germinate and grow directly on the filter medium, eventually releasing new spores back into the air. The AirPro's UV-C function neutralizes captured spores, breaking this cycle and ensuring the filter remains a permanent trap rather than a potential mold incubator.

## How Mold Affects Your Health

Mold exposure affects people differently depending on sensitivity and duration:

- **Allergic reactions** including sneezing, runny nose, red eyes, and skin rash are the most common response to mold spores.
- **Respiratory issues** such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath affect people with and without pre-existing conditions.
- **Immune compromise** is a concern with prolonged exposure, particularly to black mold mycotoxins, especially for young children, elderly individuals, and those with weakened immune systems.
- **Chronic sinusitis** has been linked to persistent mold exposure in indoor environments.

Removing mold spores from the air does not replace remediation of visible mold growth, but it significantly reduces the health impact of airborne exposure while remediation is planned or underway.
