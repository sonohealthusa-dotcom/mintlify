HEPA 14 is the highest filtration grade available in consumer air purifiers, and very few models actually meet the standard. This guide explains what HEPA 14 means, what to look for when shopping, and why the SonoHealth AirPro is one of the strongest options on the market.

## What Is HEPA 14?

HEPA 14 is a classification defined by the European standard EN 1822. A HEPA 14 filter must capture **99.995% of particles at 0.3 microns** -- the most penetrating particle size (MPPS). For comparison:

| Grade | Efficiency at 0.3 &micro;m |
|---|---|
| HEPA 11 | 95% |
| HEPA 13 ("True HEPA") | 99.95% |
| **HEPA 14** | **99.995%** |

Most consumer air purifiers use HEPA 13 or lower. HEPA 14 filters are typically found in hospitals, cleanrooms, and pharmaceutical facilities.

## Why HEPA 14 Matters

- **Ultrafine particles:** Wildfire smoke, diesel exhaust, and certain allergens produce particles well below 1 micron. HEPA 14 catches what HEPA 13 lets through.
- **Virus-sized particles:** Many airborne viruses range from 0.06 to 0.14 microns. While no filter guarantees virus removal, HEPA 14's efficiency at 0.1 microns is significantly higher than lower grades.
- **Peace of mind:** If you are buying an air purifier for health reasons, the difference between 99.95% and 99.995% efficiency is meaningful over months of continuous use.

## What to Look For in a HEPA 14 Air Purifier

Use this checklist when evaluating any HEPA 14 model:

- **Verified HEPA 14 rating** -- look for EN 1822 compliance, not just "True HEPA" marketing.
- **Multi-stage filtration** -- a pre-filter extends HEPA life; activated carbon handles gases and odors.
- **UV-C sterilization** -- adds a second layer of germicidal protection after filtration.
- **Room coverage** -- match the purifier's rated coverage to your largest room.
- **Noise level** -- anything under 30 dB is whisper-quiet for bedrooms.
- **Filter replacement cost** -- check ongoing costs, not just the sticker price.
- **Warranty length** -- 2 years minimum; longer is better.
