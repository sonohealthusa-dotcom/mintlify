## MistPro's Key Advantages

### 1. FDA Approved at $69

FDA approval is the gold standard for US medical device regulatory clearance. At $69, MistPro offers FDA-approved performance at a price competitive with many uncleared or lower-certified alternatives. Many generic mesh nebulizers on Amazon at $20–40 carry no FDA authorization — a significant distinction for patients using prescribed medications.

### 2. All-Ages Design with Included Masks

Most competitor mesh nebulizers are designed primarily for adults. The MistPro includes **both adult and child masks** in the box, making it a complete family solution without requiring separate accessories.

### 3. Physician Training Program

SonoHealth includes a **free video training series by Dr. Carolina Melgar, MD** — a certified physician — specifically for MistPro users. No comparable peer-reviewed training resource is offered by most competitors as part of the product package.

### 4. 60-Day Free Trial

The MistPro comes with a **60-day return policy with free return shipping and full refund** — one of the most generous trial periods in the nebulizer category. Most competitors offer standard 30-day returns with restocking fees.

### 5. 2-Year Warranty

The 2-year warranty on MistPro matches the best in class (PARI) and exceeds many competitors offering only 1-year coverage.

## When Competitors May Be Preferred

**PARI Trek S:** The PARI Trek S is a jet nebulizer (not mesh) with a strong clinical reputation. Preferred by some pulmonologists for specific medications (particularly for patients with cystic fibrosis, where PARI's PARI LC Plus nebulizer system is the standard of care). For general home use, MistPro's mesh technology is more convenient.

**Omron MicroAir NE-U22:** A well-regarded mesh nebulizer with strong retail distribution. MistPro's primary advantages over Omron are the included all-ages masks, physician training program, and 60-day return vs Omron's typically shorter return window.

**Generic Amazon options:** Suitable only for price-sensitive use cases where FDA status is not a concern (e.g., saline-only use). Not appropriate for prescribed medications without regulatory verification.
