If you or a family member needs breathing treatments, a portable nebulizer can make therapy faster, quieter, and possible anywhere. But devices vary widely in technology and quality. This guide covers the features that matter most so you can choose well.

<Note>
A nebulizer is a drug-delivery device. It does not include medication, and most respiratory medications require a prescription. Always use physician-prescribed or physician-approved solutions, and follow your doctor's instructions.
</Note>

## Mesh, jet, or ultrasonic — which technology is best?

The three main types differ in how they turn liquid into mist. **Vibrating mesh** nebulizers like the [MistPro](/nebulizer/how-it-works) push medication through a fine mesh, are near-silent, pocket-sized, and battery-powered. **Jet (compressor)** nebulizers are larger, louder, and need a wall outlet. **Ultrasonic** units can heat medication, which may degrade some drugs. Our [mesh vs jet](/nebulizer/mesh-vs-jet) page compares them in depth.

## Why does particle size matter?

The therapeutic benefit of inhaled medication depends on where particles land in the airways. Particles in the **1–5 micron** range reach the small airways and alveoli where asthma and COPD medications need to go. The MistPro produces particles predominantly in this range — see [how it works](/nebulizer/how-it-works) for the science.

## How fast and how quiet should it be?

Treatment time and noise matter for daily use and for children. A mesh nebulizer like the MistPro delivers a treatment in about **5–7 minutes** and runs near-silently, compared with the 15–20 minutes and loud compressor of a typical jet unit. Quieter, faster treatments are easier for kids and for nighttime use.

## What about battery, portability, and what's included?

Look for a rechargeable battery (the MistPro charges via **USB-C**) so you can treat in bed, in the car, or while [traveling](/nebulizer/travel-use). Check what comes in the box — the MistPro includes an adult mask, a child mask, a mouthpiece, a charging cable, and a case, which covers the whole family.

## Is FDA status important?

Yes. Choose an **FDA-cleared or FDA-approved** device rather than an unregulated import. The MistPro is FDA-approved as a medical device — see [FDA approval](/nebulizer/fda-approval). Regulation provides assurance about safety and consistent performance with prescribed medications.

## Buyer's checklist

- Vibrating mesh technology (quiet, portable, no medication heating)
- 1–5 micron particle size for deep lung delivery
- Fast treatments (about 5–7 minutes)
- Rechargeable USB-C battery
- Masks (adult + child) and mouthpiece included
- FDA-cleared/approved and easy to [clean](/nebulizer/cleaning-guide)

<Warning>
A nebulizer is not a rescue device for a severe attack on its own, and it cannot replace emergency care. If breathing is severe, lips or face look blue, or a rescue inhaler isn't helping, call emergency services. See [when to seek urgent care](/nebulizer/asthma-relief).
</Warning>
