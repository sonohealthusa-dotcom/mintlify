## Magnesium Glycinate

Magnesium glycinate is magnesium bound to glycine, a calming amino acid. This chelated form is absorbed via amino acid transport pathways, meaning it is highly bioavailable and bypasses the saturable mineral absorption channels.

**Best for:**
- Sleep difficulty or insomnia
- Anxiety and stress
- Muscle cramps and spasms
- People with sensitive stomachs (very gentle)
- Athletes needing overnight muscle recovery

**Why glycine matters:** Glycine is an inhibitory neurotransmitter that activates GABA receptors, reducing nervous system excitability. Taking magnesium glycinate delivers both magnesium *and* calming glycine simultaneously.

## Magnesium Citrate

Magnesium citrate is magnesium bound to citric acid. It is one of the most commonly used forms due to its good bioavailability and wide availability. At higher doses it has a laxative effect, which is why it is used in bowel preparation products (like MiraLAX alternatives).

**Best for:**
- Constipation or irregular digestion
- General magnesium replenishment
- Migraine prevention (moderate evidence)
- Budget-conscious supplementation

**Watch out for:** At doses above 350–400mg of citrate specifically, it can cause loose stools. At the blended serving size in Spectrum 5, the citrate component is a fraction of the total 500mg, so GI effects are minimal.
