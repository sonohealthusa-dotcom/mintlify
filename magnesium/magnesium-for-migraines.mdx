Magnesium is one of the most discussed nutrients for migraine, and people who get frequent headaches often ask whether a supplement could help. The research is promising for some, but it's important to set realistic expectations.

<Note>
Spectrum 5 is a dietary supplement, not a migraine treatment. These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. Recurring, severe, or "worst-ever" headaches should always be evaluated by a doctor.
</Note>

## Is there a link between magnesium and migraines?

Research has found that some people who experience migraines tend to have lower magnesium levels, and magnesium is involved in nerve signaling and blood-vessel function that play a role in headache. Because of this, magnesium is sometimes discussed as part of a preventive routine — though results vary from person to person.

## Which type of magnesium is used?

Well-absorbed, gentle forms are generally preferred for daily use. Spectrum 5 combines [five forms of magnesium](/magnesium/ingredients) — glycinate, malate, citrate, oxide, and hydroxide — providing 500mg per serving. Chelated forms like glycinate are valued for being [easy on the stomach](/magnesium/magnesium-types-guide).

## Can it help menstrual or hormonal headaches?

Some people connect headaches to their cycle, and magnesium is one nutrient discussed for premenstrual symptoms generally. Evidence is mixed, so it's best viewed as one supportive piece rather than a cure. A healthcare provider can help with a tailored plan for hormonal or menstrual migraine.

## How long before I'd notice a difference?

Supplements that affect headache frequency are typically taken consistently for several weeks before judging any benefit. Take Spectrum 5 daily with food, ideally at a [consistent time](/magnesium/dosage-guide), and track your headaches to see whether anything changes.

## When to see a doctor

See a doctor for headaches that are new, frequent, severe, or different from your usual pattern, or that come with neurological symptoms. Magnesium is not a substitute for evaluation or prescribed migraine care — review [who should take it](/magnesium/who-needs-it) and [safety cautions](/magnesium/safety) first.
