## I'm Getting Loose Stools or Digestive Discomfort

This is the most common side effect of magnesium supplementation and is usually dose-dependent.

**Fixes:**
- **Take with food** — taking magnesium on an empty stomach significantly increases GI sensitivity. Always take Spectrum 5 with a meal.
- **Start with 1 capsule** — if you are new to magnesium supplementation, start with 1 capsule daily for the first week, then increase to the full 2-capsule serving.
- **Split the dose** — take 1 capsule with breakfast and 1 capsule with dinner instead of both at once.
- **Give it time** — digestive sensitivity often resolves within 1–2 weeks as your body adjusts.

<Note>
Magnesium oxide is the form most associated with a laxative effect at high doses. Spectrum 5 uses oxide as one of five forms — at the blended dose, the contribution from oxide is moderated. If you remain sensitive, stick with the split-dose approach.
</Note>

## I'm Not Noticing Any Results

**Timeline expectations:**
- Sleep improvement: Most people notice a difference within 1–2 weeks
- Muscle cramp reduction: Often within 1 week
- Stress and anxiety reduction: Typically 2–4 weeks
- Energy and fatigue: 3–6 weeks of consistent daily use

Magnesium works by replenishing cellular stores — this takes time. Blood serum magnesium is only ~1% of total body magnesium; the rest is stored in bones and soft tissue. Stores deplete slowly and replenish slowly.

**If you see no improvement after 6 weeks:**
- Confirm you are taking 2 capsules (500mg) daily consistently
- Make sure you are taking with food
- Consider whether other factors (sleep hygiene, hydration, stress) are the primary driver of your symptoms

## The Capsules Are Hard to Swallow

- Take with a full glass of water
- Try taking with food — the swallowing reflex is stronger when eating
- You can open the capsule and mix the powder into a smoothie or yogurt — the taste is mild and earthy

## Can I Take More Than 2 Capsules?

The recommended dose is 2 capsules (500mg) daily. The NIH Tolerable Upper Intake Level for supplemental magnesium is 350mg/day (from supplements, not including dietary magnesium). Spectrum 5 at 500mg exceeds this threshold.

The UL is set based on laxative effects at high doses — not toxicity. Many adults tolerate doses above 350mg without issue. However, do not exceed the recommended dose without consulting your physician, particularly if you have kidney disease.

## I Have Kidney Disease — Can I Take Spectrum 5?

**Consult your physician before use.** People with kidney disease or impaired renal function may not excrete excess magnesium efficiently, which can lead to elevated serum magnesium levels (hypermagnesemia). Spectrum 5 is not recommended for people with significant kidney impairment without medical supervision.

## Spectrum 5 Interacts with My Medication

Magnesium can interfere with the absorption of certain medications. Take Spectrum 5 at least 2 hours apart from:

- Antibiotics (tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones)
- Bisphosphonates (osteoporosis medications)
- Thyroid medications (levothyroxine)

Magnesium may also enhance the effects of:
- Muscle relaxants
- Blood pressure medications (mild potentiation)

Always inform your physician about all supplements you are taking.

## The Capsules Have an Unusual Smell

Magnesium supplements — particularly blends containing oxide and hydroxide — can have a mild mineral or earthy smell. This is normal and not a sign of spoilage. Spectrum 5 does not contain artificial fragrances.
