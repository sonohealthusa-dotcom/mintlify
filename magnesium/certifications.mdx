## GMP-Certified Manufacturing

**GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice)** is a set of regulations enforced by the FDA under 21 CFR Part 111 that governs the manufacture, processing, labeling, and holding of dietary supplements. GMP certification means:

- Raw materials are tested for identity, purity, strength, and composition before use
- Manufacturing processes are documented and consistently followed
- Finished products are tested before release
- Facilities are regularly audited for cleanliness, equipment calibration, and process control
- Batch records are maintained for traceability

Spectrum 5 is manufactured in a **USA-based, FDA-registered, GMP-certified facility**. This ensures every bottle meets the same quality standard as the last.

## Third-Party Testing

Every production batch of Spectrum 5 undergoes **independent third-party laboratory testing** to verify:

- **Ingredient identity** — confirms that each of the five magnesium forms is present
- **Potency** — verifies that the 500mg magnesium per serving label claim is accurate
- **Purity** — screens for heavy metals, microbial contamination, and environmental contaminants
- **Freedom from adulterants** — confirms the absence of undeclared ingredients

Third-party testing is conducted by accredited labs independent of SonoHealth. This is a higher standard than in-house testing alone, because results cannot be influenced by the manufacturer.

## What "Made in the USA" Means for Supplements

For dietary supplements, "Made in the USA" means the product is manufactured and assembled within the United States. This has important implications:

- The facility is subject to FDA inspection and enforcement
- Manufacturing must comply with 21 CFR Part 111 GMP regulations
- Supply chain traceability is held to US regulatory standards
- Consumers have legal recourse under US consumer protection law

## Label Claims: What's Absent Matters

Spectrum 5 intentionally contains no:
- **Magnesium stearate** — a common lubricant in tablets that some studies suggest may reduce absorption and biofilm integrity
- **Titanium dioxide** — a whitening agent flagged by EFSA for potential genotoxicity
- **Artificial colorants or dyes** — no FD&C colors
- **Talc or silicon fillers** beyond trace silicon dioxide as a flow agent
- **Gluten or soy** — relevant for the estimated 1-3% of the US population with celiac disease or soy sensitivity
