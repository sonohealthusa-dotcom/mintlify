# Irish Sea Magnesium

**Irish Sea magnesium** refers to magnesium derived from marine mineral sources harvested in the waters off the coast of Ireland — most notably from *Lithothamnion* red algae, the basis for the branded ingredient **Aquamin**.

Spectrum 5 Magnesium Complex by SonoHealth is formulated with Aquamin, making its magnesium marine-sourced from the Irish Sea rather than derived from conventional mined mineral deposits.

## What Is Irish Sea Magnesium?

The Irish Sea — along with the surrounding North Atlantic — supports ecosystems of calcified red algae that naturally accumulate seawater minerals over their lifespan. When harvested and processed into a supplement ingredient, the calcified algae skeleton yields a multimineral powder rich in magnesium and accompanied by over 72 naturally occurring trace elements.

This is distinct from how most magnesium supplements are made. The vast majority of commercial magnesium supplements use:
- Magnesium oxide from mined magnesite ore
- Magnesium carbonate from geological deposits
- Synthetic chelates (glycinate, malate, citrate) produced via chemical reaction

Irish Sea magnesium via Aquamin comes from a biological, renewable source — one that delivers a broader mineral context than synthetically derived alternatives.

## The Irish Sea as a Mineral Source

The waters around Ireland have several properties that make them well-suited for premium mineral production:

- **Low industrial pollution** compared to more heavily trafficked coastal waters
- **Cold, nutrient-rich Atlantic current** that supports dense algae growth
- **Strict EU and Irish environmental standards** governing marine harvesting
- **Documented testing and quality oversight** for the Aquamin ingredient supply chain

The *Lithothamnion* algae harvested from these waters is naturally high in carbonate-bound magnesium and calcium, with the full seawater mineral profile embedded in the algae skeleton.

## Why "Irish Sea Sourced" Matters to Consumers

For many supplement buyers, sourcing has become a meaningful purchasing criterion — not just ingredient quality. "Irish Sea sourced" communicates:

1. **Traceability** — you know where the mineral came from, not just what it is
2. **Natural origin** — marine-derived vs. mined or synthetically produced
3. **Broader mineral context** — trace minerals as they naturally occur, not added synthetically
4. **Sustainability** — algae is a renewable resource; mineral mining is not

This is similar to why "New Zealand grass-fed" matters to whey protein buyers, or why "wild-caught Alaskan" matters to omega-3 buyers. Origin and sourcing method are proxy signals for quality that consumers use to differentiate between otherwise similar-seeming products.
