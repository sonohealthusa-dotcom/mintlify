## Dosage & Timing

**When should I take Spectrum 5?**
Take with a meal for best absorption and to minimize any GI sensitivity. For sleep benefits, take 30–60 minutes before bed. For muscle recovery, within 30 minutes post-workout is optimal. For general health, any consistent time with food works.

**Can I take more than 2 capsules per day?**
The serving size is 2 capsules (500mg). This exceeds the Tolerable Upper Intake Level (UL) for supplemental magnesium (350mg/day) set by the NIH. Consult your doctor before taking higher doses, particularly if you have kidney issues.

**How long before I notice results?**
Most users notice sleep and relaxation improvements within 1–2 weeks. Full benefits — including muscle recovery improvements and energy stabilization — typically develop over 4–6 weeks of consistent daily use.

**Can I take Spectrum 5 with other supplements?**
Yes, in most cases. It combines well with Vitamin D3, B vitamins, and general multivitamins. If taking calcium supplements, separate by a few hours to allow independent absorption. If taking zinc, staggering doses is also recommended as they compete for some of the same transporters.

## Medical Questions

**Can I take Spectrum 5 if I'm pregnant?**
Consult your OB/GYN first. Magnesium supplementation during pregnancy has well-documented benefits (reduces leg cramps, may reduce preeclampsia risk), but dosing and supervision are important.

**Is Spectrum 5 safe with kidney disease?**
People with chronic kidney disease (CKD stage 3+) should not supplement with magnesium without medical supervision, as impaired kidneys cannot efficiently excrete excess magnesium.

**Does magnesium interact with my blood pressure medication?**
Magnesium may have an additive blood pressure-lowering effect. Inform your prescriber that you are supplementing with magnesium.

**Can I take Spectrum 5 with antibiotics?**
Yes, but separate the doses. Magnesium binds to tetracyclines and quinolone antibiotics and reduces their absorption. Take Spectrum 5 at least 2 hours before or after your antibiotic.
