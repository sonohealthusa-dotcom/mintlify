## What Does "72 Trace Minerals" Actually Mean?

Seawater contains virtually every mineral element found on earth — most in trace amounts. The *Lithothamnion* red algae used to produce Aquamin absorbs these seawater minerals throughout its lifespan, concentrating them in its calcified skeleton.

When Aquamin is processed, this full seawater mineral spectrum is preserved. The result: over 72 naturally occurring trace elements, including:

**Major supporting minerals:** Boron, zinc, iron, copper, manganese, selenium, silicon, chromium, iodine

**Dozens more at trace concentrations:** Vanadium, nickel, molybdenum, cobalt, and others at naturally occurring parts-per-million or parts-per-billion levels

These aren't added synthetically at specific doses — they're naturally present from the ocean environment, at concentrations proportional to their abundance in seawater. This is exactly how trace minerals appear in mineral-rich whole foods.

## The Broader Mineral Context Story

Magnesium supplements are typically consumed because someone is deficient in or not getting enough magnesium. But magnesium works in biological systems alongside other minerals — not alone.

Boron appears to help the body retain magnesium (reducing urinary excretion). Zinc co-regulates stress pathways that magnesium also modulates. Manganese shares mitochondrial enzyme activity with magnesium. These relationships don't require high doses of trace minerals — they're optimized by having the minerals present at all, which is what Aquamin's naturally co-occurring trace element profile provides.
