## What Is a Magnesium Complex?

A magnesium complex is a supplement that delivers magnesium through two or more distinct molecular forms — such as glycinate, malate, citrate, oxide, or hydroxide — rather than relying on a single form.

Each form of magnesium:
- Has a different absorption rate and bioavailability
- Is carried to tissues by different transport mechanisms
- Targets different physiological functions

A well-designed complex works across multiple absorption pathways simultaneously, increasing total uptake and addressing a wider range of health needs than any single form can achieve.

## Why Most Magnesium Supplements Fall Short

The majority of magnesium supplements on the market are single-form products — most commonly magnesium oxide or magnesium citrate. The problem:

| Common Single-Form Product | What It Misses |

| Magnesium glycinate only | Excellent for calm/sleep; no muscle energy or digestive support |

Single-form magnesium supplements require the consumer to research and stack multiple products to cover the full range of magnesium's roles in the body — or simply accept partial coverage.

## The Five Forms and What Each One Does

**Magnesium Glycinate** — Highest bioavailability; supports sleep onset, calm, and anxiety reduction via glycine's inhibitory neurotransmitter activity.

**Magnesium Malate** — Bound to malic acid, a Krebs cycle intermediate; supports ATP (cellular energy) production and reduces muscle soreness.

**Magnesium Citrate** — One of the most studied forms; supports metabolic health, mood via serotonin pathways, and kidney health.

**Magnesium Oxide** — Highest elemental magnesium percentage by weight; associated with migraine prevention and sleep improvement in clinical research.

**Magnesium Hydroxide** — Supports digestive regularity, bowel motility, and acid balance.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a magnesium complex better than magnesium glycinate alone?**
For sleep specifically, magnesium glycinate is highly effective on its own. For broader health goals — energy, muscle function, digestion, and stress — a complex that includes glycinate alongside other forms covers more ground.

**How much magnesium should a complex contain per serving?**
The RDA for magnesium is 310–420mg for adults. A well-designed complex should deliver at least 300mg, with 400–500mg being ideal for people supplementing to address active deficiency.

**What are cofactor trace minerals in a magnesium complex?**
Trace minerals like boron, zinc, selenium, and manganese support enzyme activity alongside magnesium. A marine-sourced magnesium complex from Aquamin includes over 70 of these naturally occurring trace minerals within the same formula.

**Can I take a magnesium complex every day?**
Yes. Magnesium is a daily dietary requirement. Most people benefit from consistent daily supplementation rather than intermittent use. Take with food to support absorption and minimize any digestive effects.
