## Why Magnesium Matters for Women's Health

Magnesium is involved in over 300 enzymatic reactions, with particular relevance to several areas commonly prioritized by women:

### Sleep Quality
Magnesium regulates melatonin production and supports GABA activity — the brain's primary calming neurotransmitter. Low magnesium is associated with difficulty falling asleep and poor sleep quality. Magnesium glycinate (one of Spectrum 5's five forms) is the form most commonly associated with improved sleep onset and quality.

### Stress and Mood
Magnesium plays a central role in regulating the HPA (hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal) axis — the stress response system. Chronic stress depletes magnesium; depleted magnesium amplifies stress reactivity. Magnesium also supports serotonin synthesis, relevant to mood balance.

### Muscle Health and Cramps
Magnesium is required for muscle relaxation (calcium triggers contraction; magnesium signals release). Many women experience muscle cramps — during menstruation, exercise, or sleep — that are associated with low magnesium status. Magnesium malate in Spectrum 5 specifically supports ATP production and muscle recovery.

### Bone Density
Magnesium is a critical cofactor in calcium metabolism and bone mineralization. Without adequate magnesium, calcium is less efficiently deposited into bone. This is particularly relevant for women, who are at higher risk for bone density loss over time.

### Hormonal Balance Support
Magnesium is involved in the synthesis and regulation of sex hormones. Adequate magnesium status may support more balanced hormonal function, though it should be understood as a foundational nutrient rather than a direct hormonal treatment.
