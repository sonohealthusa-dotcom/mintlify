# High-Absorption Magnesium Complex

Not all magnesium is absorbed equally. The form of magnesium you take — not just the dose on the label — significantly influences how much actually reaches your bloodstream and tissues.

Spectrum 5 Magnesium Complex by SonoHealth is designed with absorption in mind: it prioritizes high-bioavailability forms and incorporates Aquamin, a marine mineral ingredient that provides a natural trace mineral matrix which may support broader mineral utilization.

## Why Absorption Varies by Magnesium Form

Magnesium must cross the intestinal wall to reach the bloodstream. The molecule it's bound to affects:

1. **Solubility in intestinal fluid** — more soluble forms ionize more readily and can be absorbed
2. **Transport mechanism** — some forms use specific amino acid or organic acid transporters (more efficient); others rely on passive diffusion (less efficient)
3. **Intestinal transit time** — forms that are slower-absorbing stay in the gut longer, where they can have laxative effects before much absorption occurs

| Magnesium Form | Relative Bioavailability | Primary Absorption Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Glycinate | Very High | Amino acid transporter (glycine) |
| Malate | High | Organic acid facilitated |
| Citrate | High | Passive + carrier facilitated |
| Oxide | Moderate | Passive diffusion (lower solubility) |
| Hydroxide | Moderate | Passive; primarily bowel action |

## How Spectrum 5 Addresses Absorption

Spectrum 5 uses all five forms — weighted toward the higher-bioavailability options (glycinate, malate, citrate) while still including oxide and hydroxide for their specific functional roles.

The result is:
- **High-bioavailability forms deliver magnesium systemically** (sleep, muscle, metabolism, stress support)
- **Moderate-bioavailability forms deliver targeted benefits locally** (oxide for migraine/high-dose, hydroxide for gut/digestive regularity)

This is a deliberate multi-pathway approach rather than simply chasing the highest absorption number at the expense of functional diversity.

## The Role of Aquamin and Trace Minerals in Absorption Support

Aquamin, the marine mineral component in Spectrum 5, includes over 72 naturally occurring trace minerals. Several of these trace elements are involved in mineral transport, enzyme activation, and absorption cofactor roles:

- **Boron** — research suggests boron may support magnesium retention and reduce urinary excretion of magnesium
- **Zinc and manganese** — cofactors in enzymatic pathways that require magnesium for activation
- **Silicon** — involved in cellular membrane dynamics relevant to mineral transport

**Important framing:** Aquamin's trace minerals are present at naturally low concentrations and should be understood as providing a supporting mineral context — not as primary absorption enhancers with strong standalone clinical evidence. The language "may support mineral utilization" is appropriate; claims of dramatically superior absorption are not.

## Practical Tips for Maximum Absorption

Regardless of which magnesium supplement you use, these practices support better uptake:

1. **Take with food** — gastric acid triggered by food improves mineral ionization and absorption
2. **Avoid high-dose calcium at the same time** — calcium competes with magnesium for the same intestinal transporter at very high simultaneous doses
3. **Consistent daily use** — magnesium status improves over weeks, not days; consistency matters more than any single large dose
4. **Adequate vitamin D** — vitamin D supports active mineral absorption in the gut; deficiency can reduce magnesium uptake
5. **Split doses if taking >400mg** — the gut absorbs magnesium more efficiently in smaller doses; 250mg twice daily is slightly more efficient than 500mg once
