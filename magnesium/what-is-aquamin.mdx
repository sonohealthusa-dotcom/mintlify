# What Is Aquamin?

**Aquamin** is a natural, marine-derived multimineral ingredient harvested from calcified red algae (*Lithothamnion* species) in the clean waters of the Irish Sea. Unlike synthetic mineral isolates or mined mineral compounds, Aquamin delivers magnesium in a naturally occurring matrix that includes over 72 trace minerals — all as they exist together in nature.

Spectrum 5 Magnesium Complex by SonoHealth uses Aquamin as its marine mineral source, distinguishing it from magnesium supplements that use earth-mined or synthetically derived magnesium compounds.

## Where Does Aquamin Come From?

Aquamin is sourced from a species of red algae called *Lithothamnion*, which grows in the unpolluted waters of the North Atlantic, primarily off the coast of Ireland. The algae naturally accumulate minerals from seawater over their lifespan. When harvested and processed, the calcified skeleton of the algae yields a mineral-rich powder containing magnesium, calcium, and a broad spectrum of co-occurring trace minerals.

The sourcing region — the Irish Sea and North Atlantic — is significant because these waters are among the cleanest in the world, supporting high-purity marine mineral production with minimal heavy metal contamination risk.

## Why Is Aquamin Different from Standard Magnesium?

Most magnesium supplements use one or more of the following synthetic or earth-mined sources:

- **Magnesium oxide** — produced by calcining magnesium carbonate from mined magnesite ore
- **Magnesium citrate** — produced by reacting magnesium carbonate with citric acid in a laboratory
- **Magnesium glycinate** — produced by chelating synthetic magnesium with glycine

These are clean, effective ingredients in their own right. But they deliver magnesium in isolation — without the broader trace mineral context that naturally occurs in food-sourced mineral complexes.

Aquamin is different because the magnesium arrives alongside a natural trace mineral matrix, reflecting the mineral environment from which the algae grew. This is a closer match to the way magnesium appears in whole foods, where it co-occurs with cofactor minerals rather than existing in isolation.
