## The FDA Clearance Gap

The most important difference is FDA regulatory status. Generic marketplace dopplers are almost universally sold without FDA clearance, meaning:

- No FDA review of ultrasound output levels
- No verification of safety or effectiveness
- Not legally authorized as a medical device in the US
- No requirement for accurate, compliant safety labeling

The FDA has specifically warned consumers about purchasing uncleared ultrasound devices, noting that unregulated output levels are a potential concern.

## Why "Same Technology" Isn't the Full Story

Generic sellers often claim their dopplers use "the same technology as clinical devices." This may be partially true in terms of Doppler ultrasound principles — but the execution matters:

- **Probe calibration:** Clinical and FDA-cleared devices are calibrated to deliver precise frequency and output. Generic probes may not be calibrated.
- **BPM accuracy:** Digital BPM readings are only useful if the algorithm is accurate. Generic devices' accuracy is unverified.
- **Build quality:** A probe that fails after a few months provides no value — and most generic sellers offer no warranty.

## When a Generic Doppler Might Be Acceptable

If budget is the primary constraint and you understand the limitations, a generic doppler may still allow you to hear a heartbeat. Many parents do use them without issue. The risks are primarily around regulatory compliance and post-purchase support — not acute safety hazards from a single, brief session.

However, for medical-adjacent use during pregnancy, FDA clearance, accurate calibration, and reliable customer support are reasonable standards to hold.
