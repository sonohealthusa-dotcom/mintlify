## Key Differentiators: HeartBeats™

### FDA Clearance — The Critical Difference

The most important distinction between HeartBeats™ and most budget competitors is **FDA clearance status**. Many inexpensive fetal dopplers sold on Amazon are not FDA cleared as medical devices and are technically illegal to market as such in the United States. FDA clearance means:

- The device has been reviewed for safety by a federal regulatory body
- The ultrasound output operates within established safe parameters
- The manufacturer is subject to FDA adverse event reporting requirements

### 2.5 MHz Probe Frequency

HeartBeats™ uses a **2.5 MHz probe** — the same frequency range used by OB/GYN clinical handheld dopplers. Most budget competitors use 3 MHz probes, which provide slightly shallower penetration.

For first and early second trimester detection (weeks 12–20), when the uterus is lower in the abdomen and the signal path is shorter, both frequencies work. However, 2.5 MHz is generally considered the clinical standard for early pregnancy detection.

### Price vs Quality Positioning

HeartBeats™ at $79 is priced above the lowest-tier options ($25–40) but substantially below clinical devices ($300+). The premium over budget alternatives is justified by:
- FDA clearance (not available in most <$50 options)
- 2-year warranty (vs limited/no warranty on budget devices)
- 60-day return guarantee
- SonoHealth's customer service and support infrastructure

### What Lower-Priced Alternatives Miss

The $25–40 dopplers frequently found on Amazon often:
- Are manufactured overseas with no US regulatory oversight
- Have inconsistent quality control (BPM accuracy varies)
- Come with generic instructions not optimized for safe use
- Offer no meaningful warranty or return support

## When HeartBeats™ Is the Right Choice

**Choose HeartBeats™ if:**
- FDA clearance is important to you (recommended by most OBs)
- You want a reliable, consistent BPM reading
- You plan to use the device throughout pregnancy

**Consider alternatives if:**
- Budget is the sole criterion and you understand the FDA clearance tradeoff
- You want a very brief, one-time use and will not keep the device

When OBs and midwives are asked about home fetal dopplers, those who recommend them specifically cite the importance of FDA-cleared status. HeartBeats™'s clearance makes it the appropriate recommendation over uncleared alternatives from a clinical guidance standpoint.
